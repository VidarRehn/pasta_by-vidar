Spaghetti Carbonara

The name Carbonara comes from the word carbonaro, which means “coal burner” in Italian. Some believe the dish was created as a hearty, easy-to-make meal by men working outdoors for long periods.

Serves: 20min
  • 350 g Spaghetti
  • 200 g Guanciale (or Pancetta)
  • 4 whole medium eggs
  • 100 g grated Pecorino
  1. Boil water for the pasta. Add a generous amount of salt to the water.
  2. Cut the Guanciale into small pieces and fry over a medium heat until cooked evenly and a little crispy.
  3. Whisk together the eggs and the Pecorino cheese. Add some coarsely ground pepper to the mix.
  4. Boil your Spaghetti until perfectly "al dente"
  5. Strain your pasta and add to your frying pan with the Guanciale.
  6. Turn off the heat and add your egg and Pecorino mix. Stir until all combined.
  7. Enjoy!

Papardelle al Ragu

The word Pappardelle comes from the Italian word pappare, which means "to gobble upp". The pasta shape originates from Tuscany

Serves: 2.5 hrs
  • 350 g Papardelle
  • 350 g chuck (beef)
  • 1 carrot
  • 1 stick of celery
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 large Shallot onion
  • 1 dl red wine
  • 1 dl beef stock
  • 1 can crushed tomatoes
  1. Roughly cut the beef chuck into pieces. Fry in oil on high heat until brown
  2. Finely dice the carrots, celery, shallots and garlic. Add to meat and fry until soft
  3. Add red wine, beef stock and crushed tomatoes. Leave to simmer under a lid on low heat for a minumium of two hours
  4. Boil water for the pasta. Add a generous amount of salt to the water.
  5. Boil your Papardelle until perfectly "al dente"
  6. Strain your pasta and add to your pot with the ragu
  7. Turn off the heat and stir until all combined.
  8. Enjoy!

Gemelli ai Funghi

The name Gemelli comes from the Italian work for "twins". This is because the shape resembles two tubes twisted around one another. However, it is in fact a single s-shaped strand twisted into a spiral

Serves: 30min
  • 350 g Gemelli
  • 350 g Porcini mushrooms
  • 1 large shallot onion
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 75 g butter
  • 100 g grated Parmeggiano cheese
  1. Boil water for the pasta. Add a generous amount of salt to the water.
  2. Slice the Porcini mushrooms and fry over medium-high heat in butter
  3. Finely dice the shallot and garlic. In a separate pan fry them together until onion is translucent.
  4. Boil your Gemelli until perfectly "al dente"
  5. Add mushrooms to the onions and also add a few table spoons of pasta water.
  6. Strain your pasta and add to your frying pan with the mushrooms and onion-mix
  7. Turn off the heat and stir until all combined.
  8. Serve with a generous amount of grated Parmeggiano, freshly chopped parsley, and coarsely ground black pepper.
  9. Enjoy!
